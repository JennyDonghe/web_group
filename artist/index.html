Nasty Nets: The Original Surf Club

Founded: 2006

Platform: Blog format using a default WordPress theme

Description: Nasty Nets called itself an “internet surfing club,” a group of artists who treated web-browsing itself as a creative act. They collected, remixed, and posted strange fragments of the web — broken GIFs, weird stock images, MySpace debris — turning the detritus of early digital life into art commentary. They coined the term “surf club” to describe this communal blogging practice: a digital salon where artists collectively posted visual finds or creations with a mix of irony, humor, and nostalgia. Their tone was intentionally flat — embracing “default aesthetics” (plain templates, clunky layouts, system fonts) to parody both the art world’s polish and the tech world’s over-designed pretensions.

Founders: Marisa Olson, Guthrie Lonergan, John Michael Boling, Joel Holmberg

Engineer: Guthrie Lonergan
