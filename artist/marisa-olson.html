Marisa Olson

Artist, writer, curator | b. 1977, Augsburg, Germany | Based in New York

Overview

Marisa Olson is a German-born, New York–based artist, writer, and curator whose work spans performance, video/new media, painting, drawing, and installation. She explores the cultural histories of technology and wellness, gendered experience, and pop participation. Her projects have been presented at major venues including the Whitney Museum, New Museum, Venice Biennale, Tate Modern, and Tate Liverpool. A founding member of the internet surf club Nasty Nets, Olson has exhibited at the Sundance and New York Underground Film Festivals. Her work has been featured in The New York Times, Frieze, Art in America, Le Monde, and Dazed, among others, and her writing appears in Artforum, e-flux, Aperture, and Wired. Formerly Editor & Curator at Rhizome and Associate Director of SF Camerawork, she has curated for the New Museum, Guggenheim, and SFMOMA. Olson has held residencies at Eyebeam and the Atlantic Center for the Arts, taught at RISD and NYU, and studied at Goldsmiths, UC Santa Cruz, and UC Berkeley.
